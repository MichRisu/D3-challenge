Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

When examining the relationship between poverty and the uninsured, there is a positive trend among the states. More uninsured people are from states who have higher percentages of poverty. We see a similar trend when comparing poverty against the percentage of smokers and poverty versus the percentage of obesity.

It is important to note that higher median household income does not necessarily indicate lower percentages in the uninsured. Some states, such as New York and California, who have relatively higher median incomes also have higher percentages of uninsured. We would need to examine other factors, such as cost of living or population to understand more.
